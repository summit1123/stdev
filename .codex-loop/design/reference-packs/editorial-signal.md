# Reference Pack: editorial-signal

Use this when the output is a proposal, PRD, strategy memo, report, or other reviewer-facing document that needs authority without decorative noise.

## Core feel

- dense and authored, not spacious and presentational
- black-first typography with one restrained accent family
- tables, comparison grids, timelines, and evidence panels over floating cards
- captions that clarify facts instead of narrating design

## Structural cues

- open sections with a strong heading and immediately useful substance
- use full-width tables when comparing options, evidence, or rollout phases
- keep visual callouts attached to content, not drifting in empty space
- when a screenshot appears, state exactly what it proves

## Good fits

- contest submissions
- policy and market background sections
- implementation proof summaries
- buyer-facing one-pagers

## Avoid

- decorative chips, circles, ribbons, or bento boxes
- slideware hero layouts
- assistant-style “this document explains” narration
- large empty page bottoms
