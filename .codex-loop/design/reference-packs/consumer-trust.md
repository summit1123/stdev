# Reference Pack: consumer-trust

Use this when the product is a consumer-facing service that must feel polished and approachable without drifting into generic AI-app aesthetics.

## Core feel

- clean, modern, and reassuring
- clear primary actions, obvious state changes, minimal visual clutter
- trust comes from good hierarchy and transparent outcomes
- references should feel product-grade, not pitch-deck-like

## Structural cues

- one clear primary action per view
- concise supporting copy near the action, not in separate explainer blocks
- highlight what will happen next, what can be undone, and what costs money
- use imagery sparingly and only when it grounds the service

## Good fits

- scheduling or booking services
- household assistants
- shopping or recommendation flows with AI support
- family or guardian-facing approval experiences

## Avoid

- purple-gradient AI branding by default
- decorative mascot-like UI pieces
- endless summary cards replacing real flow
- hidden fees, limits, or action consequences
